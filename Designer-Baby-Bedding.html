Designer Baby Bedding:
Going OUTSIDE the Box...
...But Staying INSIDE the Budget


Find this trendy design by Dwell Studio at Layla Grace.
Hello.

My name is Heather...

...and I like designer baby bedding.

I can't help it. In fact, I'm just the teensiest-weensiest jealous of the octuplet mom...just THINK of all the crib bedding she got to pick out!

MAN, I'd love to be the friend to help with that shopping.

(Notice I didn't say "the mom"...my mothering limit is a lot smaller than 8!)

Can we all just admit it? Can we all just fess up to the fact that picking out infant bedding is one of the major perks of having a baby?

Since my babies are all several years apart, I've made it a tradition to pick out a new bedding set with each child. Why? Because, first of all, I'm a self-proclaimed crib-natic (yes, I made that word up. think "fanatic about cute cribs".)

Secondly, because I have this wierd hormonal thing that takes over with each pregnancy (sound familiar?) and convinces my more-sane brain-side that without a new set, this clueless newborn won't know how special she is. (Do pity my husband.)

And since she's going to be wearing hand-me-downs until she's twenty, I have convinced myself that we (as her parents) have some moral obligation to let the first thing her little bum rests on be new.

Is this irrational? Absolutely. Ridiculous? Perhaps.

Important for my psychological adjustment to "Next Baby"? BEYOND A DOUBT.

Finally (and by-far the most reasonable reason), because my kids are ruthless with their baby bedding sets. They drool. They slobber. They leak. They spit up. It ain't pretty.

And after 2 years of stains, tears, and chews,
my infant bedding is begging me to donate to a "kinder, gentler" home.

Now, granted, with my first baby, when things were particularly tough, that "new set" came through a massive clearance sale and extra coupon codes...but who cares?

It's possible to avoid paying "designer" costs for your designer baby bedding and no one needs to know. On the other hand, if you can afford not to scrimp on quality, there are some pretty amazing (and expensive) designer baby bedding sets out there to choose from.

Now that my third baby is growing away inside, I'm on the prowl for a new set. Here are several stores I've turned to in the past, and some new ones I'm contemplating for my next "collection". (Edit Note: Baby #3 has arrived! See photos here.)

If you're just beginning to set up your nursery, make sure to review my tips to make your baby's bedroom the safest place in your house. After all, it's the one place in your entire house where he will spend a significant amount of unsupervised time!

Personally, I recommend ordering your bedding near the beginning of your last trimester (if not earlier). Not only will this give the set time to arrive, if your little one decides he's through with your uterus and comes out early, you won't be left scrambling for bedding.

Side Note: Many of the bedding pictures in these boutiques show cribs with their side panels removed. This is just to showcase the bedding, and is not a recommendation of use! Also keep in mind that what you see is not always what you get. Read the bedding descriptions to see what's included in the standard price and what you have to pay extra for.

A Word About Ratings...

You may notice that the lowest rating I've given any of these is 3 stars. Why? Because if a store deserves only 1 or 2 stars, it doesn't deserve mentioning (nor your visit). 3-star rated stores are still worth a visit, just with a few precautions.

Baby Earth

Things You'll Love

Baby Earth does a great job in the delicate balancing act of "organic" and "not-boring". Unlike many other organic shops, their bedding ranges from bright bold colors and patterns, to more muted "natural-looking" bedding sets. Also unique to Baby Earth, they have the first bumper-free designed crib set I've seen. Many parents are wary about putting bumpers on their crib sets. If that's you, the "Babylicious Island" set provides a similiar style, bumper-free. I've pictured it here.

Besides looking around at sets, their website has given me nursery decorating ideas as well. Most sets are displayed in a nursery setting, and I love "stealing" their trendy and beautiful designs. Click here to peek at their beautiful crib bedding sets.

Designer Baby Bedding:
Bumper Crop Baby

Things You'll Love


One of Suzette's Original Creations.
Bumper Crop Baby is bedding straight from the needle to your nursery. Founder and owner Suzette assists you in creating your own 100% unique design and look. She specializes in the can't-find-exactly-what-I-had-in-mind-crowd.

She doesn't sell fabric - which means you choose any fabric you want. She doesn't force you to choose from "Style A, Style B, or Style C", but really just throws the door open and lets you dream. You tell her the things you like and she turns it into a reality. For sewing-impaired Muggles like me, it feels like pure magic.

Suzette is a fellow mom like us who hails from Missouri and loves to sew. In fact, she's passionate about sewing. She would LOVE to help you find the perfect designer baby bedding for your infant. Check out Bumper Crop Baby's amazing gallery and see what she can do. Let me assure you...it's pretty darn impressive.

Final Thoughts...

Hmmm...what can I complain about...Cost? Not really. Rather than charge you a flat fee for the bedding, she shares her labor costs (she does have a family to help feed) and then you pay for the fabric. Unless you're ordering hand-woven silks from the Emperor's personal silkworms in The Forbidden City, you'll most likely come out ahead.

Quality? Can't complain there. In fact, I've been so impressed with Suzette's beautiful handiwork and L-O-N-G happy customer list, that I asked her to sponsor one of my giveaways. (Which she did, by the way, 'cause she's nice.)

Designer Baby Bedding:
The Land of Nod

Things You'll Love


{All Creatures Great and Small}
Many of the bedding sets at The Land of Nod have a cozy quilted feel that gives the appearance of being homemade. (Go ahead...impress your mother-in-law...I won't tell...).

They have a healthy mixture of picture- and pattern-focused prints. I especially like that their picture-based prints are not overly commercialized. (I'm not a fan of Disney-themed crib sets. I promise, you won't find those here...)

Unlike many other bedding boutiques, Land of Nod sells each item separately. This allows you to choose the exact items you want and leave the rest. This is especially helpful for mothers on a strict baby budget!

Stretch that budget even further with coupon codes. Or, go directly to look around The Land of Nod.

Designers and Brands

Organics are available. All the bedding found at Land of Nod is designed by individual designers like Susan Lazar and Jill McDonald (among others) and is sold under the Land of Nod brand name. This means that all their bedding is exclusively found at their store. You will only find these bedding sets at Land of Nod.

Final Thoughts...

The Land of Nod is known for great design, and they don't disappoint in the realm of baby bedding. They only scored a 3 out of 5 for their clothing selection (see clothing reviews here), but they've moved up for bedding.

They also very cost-effective. You can pick and choose which pieces you can afford at that moment and add the rest of the pieces later (if ever).
